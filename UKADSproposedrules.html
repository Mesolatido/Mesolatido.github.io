I.              Introduction

Introduction for use before there is a network.

Welcome to the Unofficial Khan Academy Server or UKADS for short. This is the first and currently the only server on the Khancord Server Network. We are a fan site to Khan Academy. As such, we aim to provide a moderated chat environment for users from the Khan Academy learning site. Almost all users are under 18 years of age, so, it’s extra important that you adhere to our server rules. We are NOT affiliated with Khan Academy itself, other than being very fond of it. If you get banned on here, that means nothing about actual KA itself.

II             Statements, rules and punishments

a)          Statements:

1)          Kindness and common sense-

All users are expected to use common sense, netiquette (internet etiquette) and kindness. If an action or content seems questionable, please refrain from the action, topic, phrase or word until you have spoken with a moderator. Please ask for a private discussion with moderator(s) about these things in the conference room channels. Additionally, note that excessive use of all caps (more than a full line once a week or more) is considered poor netiquette.

2)          Hate speech

UKADS does not discriminate on the basis of race, religion (creed), gender, age, or disability. Hate speech and symbols are strictly prohibited. Tolerance of users of other religions is expected at all times.

3)          KA Fan site

This is a fan site to Khan Academy, as such, user’s speech here should not disrespect Khan Academy itself, for example discussing cheating on KA, or KA’s staff development areas. Additionally, trying to take a dispute with a user on Discord onto Khan Academy itself is grounds to be permanently banned from not only this server, but any Khancord network servers that may come in the future. Also, a ban on UKADS has no bearing on your standing on KA itself.

b)          Rules

1)          No NSFW

2)          Only light and readable spam is allowed and must go in the spam channel. There especially should be no spamming in the general channel. Spamming bot commands can “break” a bot. Do not use bot commands outside of the bot channel-especially in general.

3)          No using @ mentions without good reason-especially @everyone, @here or @roles, as they ping multiple users. You SHOULD @moderator, if an activity happening calls for moderator attention.

4)          No cursing-Cursing includes censored and letter cursing any time “F bomb” is involved. Also, “S bomb” and H-E-double hockey sticks, if spelled out and used as a curse, rather than the opposite of heaven. As long as not frequent- crap, darn, heck are permitted.

5)          No server advertising or enticing. We cannot endorse the content of servers not on the Khancord network. Do not post invite links, speak about your server in a way meant to entice other users to it or use the chat to fish for other users. Discussing bot support servers is acceptable.

6)          Impersonation is prohibited. Do not use the name or avatar that another user has used on any Khancord network server in the previous month. There may be some leeway for meme-related names and avatars, but it’s best to ask the user who last used it and/or a moderator before proceeding. If a moderator tells you to change your name, nickname or avatar and it doesn’t seem obvious why, this may be why.

7)          No user may use two accounts (alts) on the same server without approval from the server owner. Approval for this is generally limited to moderation staff who may need to test something. If any user happens to have permission to use an alt and uses it maliciously, they risk action to all screen names. (Common sense is required).

8)          Don’t ask other users personal information like their age, real names or location beyond time zone. Do not share this information about other users, unless it is available publicly (for example on their KA profile). Users are strongly advised not to share their last names.

9)          No drama- Examples of drama include, but are not limited to:

  • Asking why someone else was banned
  • There is a mod-log on UKADS, if it’s not listed there, do not ask
  • If a Khancord Network Server doesn’t have a log, you may ask in conference

  • Not respecting moderators
  • Criticizing  a moderator in open server
  • Moderators may ask you to follow rules that have been removed from the rules
  • If a mod tells you to stop, stop-if you think they are abusing power, talk to the server owner AFTER, but you don’t get to disobey because you don’t like what a moderator tells you.

  • Picking arguments-
  • Trying to discuss contentious political topics
  • Bringing disputes from Direct Messages to open server
  • Bringing disputes from Discord to KA itself
  • Bringing disputes from other servers
  • Spreading mean gossip about users
  • Using open chat to attempt to talk to someone that blocked you on DMs
  • Defending users who are breaking rules
  • Attempting to spread hatred towards this servers’ moderators in an outside location

c)          Discipline

Possible punishments include, but are not limited to:

  • Informal warning
  • Deletion of your comments,
  • Nick name locking
  • Role removal,
  • Formal warning with or without a warned role,
  • Softban/mute
  • Kick
  • Short term ban
  • Long term ban
  • Permaban (banned permanently)

The moderation staff on the network servers will determine the punishment for that server. In extreme cases, users may be banned from the entire Khancord Network if 75% or more of the representatives vote for this or for trying to bring Discord drama onto KA itself.

III.             User guide/terminologies:

@mention-When you @mention, people get a red notification (ping), so if you put @everyone all users get that notification. If you use @here, all users online at the time, including invisible users, will receive a notification of your incoming message.

Admin- On UKADS, the admins used to be called guardians to echo KA itself. However, this often resulting in users asking WHICH kind of guardian was being referred to, so it has been changed to administrator/admin. Admins can change the settings on the channels, ban and are generally more involved in decision making like writing the rules.

Bot- a special “user” that responds to commands. A public bot or 3rd party bot is a bot written by another user on Discord outside of our social network that comes from a published list of bots online (examples: Mee6, Tatsumaki, Aethex, Blarg bot and Dyno bot). A userbot was written by one of our users, other than the administration team.Tucker and Eytoucan are the UKADS bots. Discord marks bots with the word BOT in a blue box after its name.

Channel-the subtopics on the left designated with a # sign. If you don’t like some channels, you can mute them. Right click and the option will come up.

Conference rooms-Servers on the Khancord network will have at least one conference room, a semi-private channel where mods and users can talk. Moderators may put a user in one of these channels to advise them on how to be a better user. Users may request to be in one of these channels to speak with moderators semi-privately. It is advisable to use the conference room over Private messaging for server related discussions so that everyone is accountable for what they say there.

Drama- We know that sometimes users don’t always agree about stuff. However, it is imperative that these disputes remain private and not blow up into open server. Therefore, when a user wants to dispute with another user with witnesses, they should ask the moderators to put the involved users into a conference room channel and try to help mediate the dispute. Please don’t take advantage of our kindness to mediate disputes.We do this as a courtesy. Vanilla Dazzle is usually the moderator who would mediate a dispute.

Mini modding-Mini modding is when a user who is not a moderator steps in and tells users they are breaking the rules, without making efforts to locate a moderator. If a user finds behavior against the guidelines, such as arguing or cursing, they should tell the users to stop, then ping @moderators. If-and only if- no moderators respond, then they may tell users what was wrong with their behavior. At no time should non-moderator users try to moderate chat when there is a moderator in the conversation.

Moderators-any users above moderators such as managers, admins, founders or co-founders are also moderators. Moderators can delete and pin messages.

NSFW-stands for Not Safe For Work (or school), refers to content you wouldn’t want a teacher, parent or boss catching you looking at. This is strictly prohibited and will get you permanently banned.

Open server-any channel that most users can see so this would exclude moderator channels or conference room channels

Ping-to cause users to get a red notification leading them to your post. User get this for the server, then when they open the server, for the channel. You can also click the @ symbol on the top left of your Discord screen to pull up your notifs and jump to them.

Procedure for promotions- All users wishing to be promoted to any moderator position will be reviewed by the existing moderation staff of that server. The first part of this process is that users should have a decent record of not making disruptions on any servers on the Khancord Network. Next, users will generally be interviewed by a current moderator or two, However, a user who has moderated in other Khan Academy environments may be reviewed based on their history as a moderator. All moderators will have the chance to give their input to the other moderators and the administrators. The final process is for the administrators to decide and notify a user. Moderators are expected to be fairly active and uphold a higher standard of an example of behavior on any server on the network.

Roles-They are tags that get attached to a user. The moderation tags carry permissions with them for actions moderators need for their responsibilities as moderators.

Server- the circular icons left of the channels indicate your servers.

Verification- Users are normally verified between a day and week from when they entered the server (usually about a day).  If the moderators know a user from another server or KA itself, they may award the verification role earlier. Moderators reserve the right to delay verification of users.
